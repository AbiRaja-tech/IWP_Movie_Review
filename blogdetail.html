Jason Momoa's Latest Dream DCU Project Comments Fuel Lobo Rumors

27 Mar 2022

Jason Momoa's recent DC Universe comments and a cryptic post from James Gunn have fueled rumors that the Aquaman actor is perhaps playing Lobo. With the launch of DC Studios, Gunn and Peter Safran are officially the bosses for Warner Bros. Discovery's superhero properties as they will be focusing on leading TV, movie, and animation development. In a recent interview, Momoa, who has played Aquaman since Batman v Superman: Dawn of Justice, suggested that the new DC management is making one of his dreams come true.

Not long after, Gunn opened up a new social media account with a Lobo tease. As a follow-up, ComicBook interviewed Momoa and directly asked him if there was any chance that the two situations were in any way connected. While Momoa wouldn't give a clear answer, he made it evident that Lobo has always been his favorite comic book character, sharing the following:

While Gunn hasn't made any follow-up statements, the ongoing theory is that Momoa is indeed somehow involved with the Lobo character, either for a TV show or a movie. However, the bigger question becomes whether or not he could actually play the iconic DC bounty hunter. Since Momoa is already playing Aquaman, a.k.a. one of the founding members of the Justice League, it's highly unlikely the DCU dream he has been talking about concerns Lobo. However, the great thing with Warner Bros. over the last few years is that they have begun launching separate continuities outside the DCU, as seen with The Batman and Joker franchises.

In the scenario that it is not a Lobo film or HBO Max show, it's extremely unclear what this might be about as far as Momoa's DCU dream goes under Gunn and Safran's management. With Henry Cavill coming back as Superman, Wonder Woman 3 still going forward, and Ben Affleck's time as Batman not necessarily being over, perhaps Momoa's DCU dream is coming to fruition in a potential Justice League 2, which would be the first time since 2017 that all of the characters would be together in a project. With the DCU going forward, it would genuinely be fun to see Justice League 2 be named the mystery project, as it's time to bring the iconic DC players together for a new adventure.
